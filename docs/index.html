Methods & Validation

Why should readers trust the treatment-effect estimates?

Study Design

Target-trial aligned observational emulation

  • Explicit time-zero definition
  • Temporally anchored covariates
  • Predefined intervention strategies and bundle combinations
  • Clinically relevant outcomes across mortality and recovery domains
  • Causal estimand specification before effect estimation
Target-Trial Alignment

Treatment assignment, baseline covariates, and outcome assessment were aligned using target-trial principles to reduce immortal time bias, treatment misclassification, and temporal ambiguity.

Why Estimability Matters

Not every clinically relevant treatment question can be reliably answered from observational data. Treatment-effect estimates were interpreted only within regions supported by adequate overlap and positivity. The atlas therefore explicitly distinguishes between estimable, limited-overlap, and non-estimable comparisons rather than extrapolating unsupported effects.

Methodological takeaway: Target-trial principles, causal machine learning, calibration assessment, and estimability diagnostics should be interpreted together when evaluating treatment-effect estimates.
